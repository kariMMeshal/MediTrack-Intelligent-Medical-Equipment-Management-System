=========================================================
AI MEDICAL EQUIPMENT MANAGEMENT - MODELS INPUT & OUTPUT
=========================================================

This project contains two distinct AI models working together to manage medical equipment. Here is exactly what goes into each model and what comes out.

---------------------------------------------------------
MODEL 1: Random Forest (General Risk Classifier)
---------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Acts as a hospital administrator doing a long-term performance review of a machine.

THE INPUT: 
The model looks at lifetime tabular statistics and financial data for a device. 
It requires the following 15 data points:
  1. Device_Type (e.g., MRI Scanner, Infusion Pump)
  2. Manufacturer
  3. Model
  4. Country (Origin)
  5. Age (Years in operation)
  6. Maintenance_Cost (Total dollars spent on maintenance)
  7. Downtime (Total hours out of service)
  8. Maintenance_Frequency (How often it gets maintained)
  9. Failure_Event_Count (Total number of historical failures)
 10. Maintenance_Class (Severity/complexity of typical maintenance)
 11. Operational_Hours_Est (Total estimated hours it has been running)
 12. Expected_Lifespan_Est (Total expected lifespan in years)
 13. MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures in hours)
 14. Cost_Per_Hour (Calculated maintenance cost per operational hour)
 15. Lifespan_Usage_Ratio (Ratio of Age vs Expected Lifespan)

THE OUTPUT: 
It outputs a long-term risk category:
  - 0 = Low Risk (Machine is reliable and cost-effective)
  - 1 = Medium Risk (Machine is showing normal wear and tear)
  - 2 = High Risk (Machine is a financial or operational liability)


---------------------------------------------------------
MODEL 2: LSTM (72-Hour Imminent Failure Predictor)
---------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Acts as a technician watching live monitoring screens to detect imminent breakdowns.

THE INPUT:
The model looks at a continuous 30-day sliding window of live IoT telemetry. 
For every device, it expects exactly 30 consecutive days of these 3 sensor readings:
  1. Simulated_Temperature_Variance (Thermal stability of the device)
  2. Simulated_Motor_Vibration_Hz (Mechanical stress levels)
  3. Simulated_Voltage_Drop (Electrical stability and power draw)

THE OUTPUT:
It outputs a binary prediction regarding the next 72 hours:
  - 0 = Safe (No failure is predicted in the next 3 days)
  - 1 = Imminent Failure (The device is predicted to fail within the next 72 hours)
  - Confidence Score (e.g., 95% confident in failure)
